01 · Executive diagnosis

The product stops the scroll.
The funnel must finish the job.

A novel six-foot open flame creates immediate interest—and immediate questions. Scale will come from answering those questions faster, more visually and with greater precision.

Camp Torch does not need to become less exciting. It needs to make the excitement believable enough to reserve.
01

Strategic mandate

Move from spectacle-led curiosity to proof-led conviction before the Kickstarter launch.

What is working

Distinctive silhouette. Strong emotional territory, social-status appeal and a coherent black/orange brand world.

What limits scale

Product comprehension takes too long. Safety is asserted more than shown. The offer is under-communicated.

Highest-leverage constraint

Not awareness. Belief depth. Curiosity must progress through comprehension, safety, utility and urgency.

03 · Website audit

Turn the pre-launch page into a belief sequence.

The page should answer five questions in order: What is it? Why should I care? Is it safe? Will it work for me? Why reserve now?

Observed issue

Primal Lighting™ is intriguing but abstract. A first-time visitor needs a concrete description before a branded category.

Why it matters

Novel products already carry a comprehension tax. Ambiguity compounds it and delays the first meaningful CTA.

Recommended action

Lead with “A modular propane campfire and six-foot flame beacon,” then introduce the Primal Lighting™ language.

Observed issue

The strongest rational differentiator is not doing enough early conversion work.

Why it matters

The transformation explains portability, flexibility and value in one visual sequence.

Recommended action

Use a short looping demonstration: bag → beacon → campfire → multi-torch basecamp.

Observed issue

Large open flame imagery creates concern that broad “safe” language cannot resolve.

Why it matters

Safety is likely the largest conversion objection and the largest reputational risk.

Recommended action

  • Show shutoff response
  • Show hose routing and stability
  • State clearance and wind limitations
  • Separate legal claims from intended use

Observed issue

The visitor may understand the $1 action without understanding its actual value.

Why it matters

Low-friction offers still need explicit benefit, refundability and scarcity.

Recommended action

State what the dollar secures, whether it is refundable, the early-bird advantage and any allocation limit.

Observed issue

Long lifestyle sequences can make a novel product feel more cinematic than understandable on mobile.

Why it matters

Paid social traffic will be predominantly mobile and impatient.

Recommended action

Use compact proof modules, sticky CTA behavior, short captions and fewer full-screen text interruptions.

05 · Audience & messaging

One product. Different reasons to believe.

The campaign should remain visually coherent while adapting the problem, proof and payoff to distinct use cases.

Overlanders

Light the remote basecamp

Values portability, reliability and the ability to create a visible gathering point after dark.

  • Trigger: remote multi-night trips
  • Objection: fuel and pack size
  • Best proof: setup + pack-down
RV & van life

Real flame without hauling wood

Values cleaner setup, storage efficiency and campsite flexibility.

  • Trigger: fire restrictions
  • Objection: campground rules
  • Best proof: qualified use guidance
Tailgaters

Become the gathering point

Values spectacle, identity and instant social energy in open-air events.

  • Trigger: game day or festival
  • Objection: transport and setup
  • Best proof: real reactions
Group-trip organizers

Make the night happen

Values a reliable centerpiece that solves lighting and atmosphere for everyone.

  • Trigger: group campsite planning
  • Objection: safety around people
  • Best proof: clearances + control
Hunting & fishing camps

A visible, practical basecamp beacon

Values long nights, outdoor utility and a repeatable camp setup.

  • Trigger: seasonal camp
  • Objection: weather and stability
  • Best proof: field demonstration
Premium gear buyers

The object everyone asks about

Values design, novelty, engineering and being early to an emerging category.

  • Trigger: Kickstarter discovery
  • Objection: build quality
  • Best proof: materials + founder detail
