Make the excitement believable.

01 · Executive diagnosis

The product stops the scroll.
The funnel must finish the job.

A novel six-foot open flame creates immediate interest—and immediate questions. Scale will come from answering those questions faster, more visually and with greater precision.

Camp Torch does not need to become less exciting. It needs to make the excitement believable enough to reserve.
01

Strategic mandate

Move from spectacle-led curiosity to proof-led conviction before the Kickstarter launch.

What is working

Distinctive silhouette. Strong emotional territory, social-status appeal and a coherent black/orange brand world.

What limits scale

Product comprehension takes too long. Safety is asserted more than shown. The offer is under-communicated.

Highest-leverage constraint

Not awareness. Belief depth. Curiosity must progress through comprehension, safety, utility and urgency.

Recommended positioning

The fire-ban campfire for people who make the night happen.

Concrete category: A modular propane fire and lighting system that converts from a six-foot beacon to a low campfire.

03 · Conversion architecture

Curiosity should become qualified Kickstarter intent.

The public journey is strongest at the top. The next version should place proof and a clear reservation value directly behind the initial flame spectacle.

Attention

Stop scroll with flame, contrast and an urgent problem.

Comprehension

Define the product and show its modular mechanism immediately.

Safety belief

Demonstrate shutoff, clearances, control and limitations.

Utility belief

Prove setup, pack-down, fuel, modes and real-world use.

Reservation

Explain the $1 benefit, refundability and early-bird advantage.

Recommended CTA: Reserve the lowest Kickstarter price for $1 — fully refundable, first access and a limited early-bird allocation.
04 · Core product mechanism

One bag. Three fires.

The three-mode system is the strongest rational differentiator and should appear within the first scroll—not midway down the page.

Mode 01

Beacon

A six-foot vertical flame designed to throw light and become a visible campsite marker.

Mode 02

Campfire

A lower central flame that creates the social circle and campfire feeling without wood.

Mode 03

Basecamp

One propane source feeding multiple torches for larger group setups and gathering spaces.

06 · Creative strategy

Test belief systems—not more versions of the same ad.

Current ads over-index on spectacle, novelty and social status. The next sprint should diversify persuasion while keeping the flame as the hook.

TERRITORY 01

The fire ban stole the night.

Frame fire restrictions as the emotional problem. Show a lifeless LED campsite transforming when Camp Torch arrives.

“Wood fires banned. The night doesn’t have to be.”
TERRITORY 02

One bag. Three fires.

Use an uninterrupted setup: bag → parts → beacon → campfire → pack-down, with time and size comparisons.

“This is what six feet of living flame packs down into.”
TERRITORY 03

Safety you can see.

Demonstrate shutoff response, hose routing, clearances and fuel control with founder or engineer narration.

“The flame is wild. The safety system isn’t.”
TERRITORY 04

Real reactions.

Bring it to a campsite, tailgate or river basecamp. Capture spontaneous questions and make documentary proof the ad.

“We brought Camp Torch to a campsite and waited.”
07 · Landing-page priorities

Five fixes before Kickstarter traffic arrives.

01

Rewrite the hero for clarity

Lead with a concrete product category, then introduce Primal Lighting™ and the emotional platform.

02

Move the three-mode demo up

Show one bag becoming Beacon, Campfire and Basecamp in the opening scroll.

03

Tighten safety language

Replace broad absolutes with qualified, precise claims and visible demonstrations.

04

Clarify the reservation offer

Explain why $1 matters, whether it is refundable and what early access secures.

05

Build a reusable proof library

Setup, shutoff, transformation, pack-down, fuel explanation and authentic reactions.

08 · 30-day action roadmap

Turn the audit into an execution sprint.

Week 1

Rebuild the first scroll

Hero, CTA hierarchy, safety language and proof modules.

Week 2

Shoot the proof library

Setup, shutoff, pack-down, transformation and real reactions.

Week 3

Launch four belief tests

Benchmark proof territories against the best current spectacle ad.

Week 4

Optimize for intent

Use paid reservations and pledge quality—not CPL alone—as the decision metric.

Operating rule: every week should create reusable proof assets for ads, landing page, email and Kickstarter—not isolated creative tests.
Bottom line

Keep “bring the fire.” Shift the next sprint from spectacle-led creative to proof-led conviction.
